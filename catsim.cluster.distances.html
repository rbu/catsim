Distance Functions¶

catsim.cluster.distances.pnorm(x, y=None, p=2)[source]¶

Calculates the p-norm for two sets of elements. The p-norm equals the Minkowski distance function and is given by:

\[||x||_p = \left( \sum_{i=1}^n |x_i|^p \right)^{\frac{1}{p}}\]

For two vectors, the p-norm is given by:

\[||x, y||_p = \left( \sum_{i=1}^n |x_i - y_i|^p \right)^{\frac{1}{p}}\]

In special cases where \(p=1\), \(p=2\) or \(p=\infty\), the p-norm is equal to the Manhattan, Euclidean and Chebyshev distances, respectively.
