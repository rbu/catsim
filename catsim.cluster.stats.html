Clustering Statistical and Validity Functions¶

catsim.cluster.stats.diameter(c, D)[source]¶

Calculates cluster diameters (the distance between the two farthest data points in a cluster)

catsim.cluster.stats.dunn(c, distances)[source]¶

Dunn index for cluster validation (the bigger, the better)

\[D = \min_{i = 1 \ldots n_c; j = i + 1\ldots n_c}\left\lbrace\frac{d \left( c_i,c_j \right)}{\max_{k = 1 \ldots n_c} \left(diam \left(c_k \right) \right)} \right\rbrace\]

where \(d(c_i,c_j)\) represents the distance between clusters \(c_i\) and \(c_j\), given by the distances between its two closest data points, and \(diam(c_k)\) is the diameter of cluster \(c_k\), given by the distance between its two farthest data points.

The bigger the value of the resulting Dunn index, the better the clustering result is considered, since higher values indicate that clusters are compact (small \(diam(c_k)\)) and far apart.

[Kov05]Kovács, F., Legány, C., & Babos, A. (2005). Cluster validity measurement techniques. 6th International Symposium of Hungarian Researchers on Computational Intelligence.
catsim.cluster.stats.mean_variance(x, clusters)[source]¶

Returns the mean variance for all clusters

\[\sigma^2 = \frac{1}{G}\sum_{g=1}^G \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1; j=i+1}^N (\mu_g - d(i, j))\]
catsim.cluster.stats.min_cluster_distances(c, D)[source]¶

Calculates the distances between the two nearest points of each cluster.

catsim.cluster.stats.variances(x, clusters)[source]¶

Calculates the variance for each cluster

\[\sigma^2_g = \sum_{i=1; j=i+1}^{N_g} (\mu_g - d(i, j))\]
